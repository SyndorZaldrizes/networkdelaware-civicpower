From Civic Learning to Civic Power is a Network Delaware civic education initiative developed through a structured research fellowship. Here's what it is and how it came together.

What This Is

From Civic Learning to Civic Power is a statewide K–12 civic education initiative developed by Network Delaware. It is built on the argument that Delaware students are not being prepared to practice democracy — only to read about it. The initiative proposes a multi-front strategy to close that gap, with specific attention to communities that existing programs have consistently failed to reach.

The work is organized around a four-front framework, a six-phase Civic Action Cycle, eight guiding equity principles, and six identified priority communities. All deliverables are grounded in primary research, CIRCLE data, and analysis of five comparison states.

The Fellowship Structure

The research and materials on this site were produced through a three-phase fellowship:

  • Phase 1 — Delaware Foundation: Landscape review of Delaware's K–12 civics standards and comparative analysis of Massachusetts, Illinois, Rhode Island, California, and New York.
  • Phase 2 — Research and Tools: Best practices memo, strategic nonviolence brief, school-community partnership toolkit, workshop implementation framework, and annotated citation library.
  • Phase 3 — Policy and Funding: K–12 civic education policy brief addressed to DDOE and the Delaware General Assembly, and modular grant narrative language for six identified funders.

A voluntary side initiative — the Delaware Student Legislature — was also developed through the fellowship as a student-run civic practice model adapted from the Maryland Student Legislature.

About Network Delaware

Network Delaware is a civic organization working to build civic capacity across Delaware communities. This fellowship was conducted under Network Delaware's civic education program.
