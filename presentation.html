The Problem

STUDENTS
LEARN
ABOUT
DEMOCRACY
RARELY
PRACTICE IT

Delaware's 2018 Civics Standards contain no statutory mandate, no dedicated funding, and no accountability structure tied to LEA implementation. A student's access to quality civic education depends on which district they attend.

The Participation anchor standard, the only one asking students to act civically rather than simply understand government, is highlighted at high school once, at grade 9, and not again before graduation.

The Core Finding

"The barrier to youth civic participation is not motivation. It is access, safety, and structured invitation."
CIRCLE, 2025 · Center for Information and Research on Civic Learning and Engagement, Tufts University

The civic engagement gap is a supply-side failure. Building the supply is what this initiative does.

The Method

THE
CIVIC
ACTION
CYCLE

Six stages. Repeating loop. The cycle does not end at action. It ends at reflection, which launches the next loop. Every session in the pilot arc maps to one stage.

01 LEARN Real issues. Real policy documents. Power mapping begins. ▶
02 ANALYZE Stakeholder mapping. Root causes. Whose voices are missing? ▶
03 ORGANIZE Collective strategy. Distributed roles. Coalition built. ▶
04 ACT Real decision-making space. Institution required to respond. ▶
05 TEACH BACK Facilitate for the next person. Become a civic multiplier. ▶
06 REFLECT What actually changed? Start the next loop smarter.

The Research

FIVE
FINDINGS
01

Decision-maker contact is the mechanism that produces civic efficacy gains, with the largest effects for Black and Latino youth.

Structural equation modeling, 903 urban students. Safety-themed projects with decision-maker engagement produced the strongest future civic commitment.

Cohen et al., 2017 / I-ACTED RCT, 2025
02

Kingian Nonviolence training improves social, cultural, and emotional outcomes in school settings.

Quasi-experimental study with high school participants. Pretest/posttest design with four-month follow-up. Reduced violence, improved learning outcomes.

Cyril, URI, 2020
03

Youth with political homes participate at dramatically higher rates across all 17 civic engagement measures.

27% of 2024 youth voters were part of an organized group, vs. 13% of non-voters. The difference is structural access, not individual motivation.

CIRCLE, 2026
04

Mandates alone reproduce inequity. Equity requires funded infrastructure and community accountability in the same vehicle.

Illinois HB 4025: a floor, not an equitable starting point. Implementation gaps were worst in under-resourced schools serving Black and Brown students.

CIRCLE HB 4025 Evaluation
05

No comparison state has codified strategic nonviolence in statute. This is Delaware's specific opening.

Six jurisdictions reviewed. None integrates strategic nonviolence as a required civic education component. The policy window is open.

Network Delaware Comparative Analysis, 2025
