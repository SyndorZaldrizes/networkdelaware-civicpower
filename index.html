The Problem

A Gap Between Knowing and Doing

Delaware's civics standards, last revised in 2018, contain no statutory mandate, no dedicated funding, and no accountability structure. The Participation anchor standard — the only standard asking students to act civically, not just know about government — is highlighted at high school only at grade 9.

"The barrier to youth civic participation is not motivation. It is access, safety, and structured invitation."

CIRCLE's 2025 research confirms the civic engagement gap is a supply-side failure. Young people want to participate. They just aren't being given the tools, the practice, or the invitation.

💡

Put simply...

Imagine learning how to swim by only reading about it. That's kind of what civics class is like for most students — lots of information, but not much practice. This initiative changes that.

The Strategy

Four Strategic Fronts,
Advancing Together

The framework advances four integrated fronts simultaneously. Your deliverables map to one or more of these fronts — and every recommendation must advance at least one.

Front 1

Statewide Policy + LEA Implementation

Strengthen Delaware's K–12 civics expectations at the state level while supporting Local Education Agencies in alignment, educator development, and implementation.

Explore →
Front 2

Community-Rooted Power Building

Establish parent and student leadership labs, youth organizing pathways, and structured feedback mechanisms that give communities real — not symbolic — influence.

Explore →
Front 3

School-Based Partnerships + Hands-On Practice

Pilot hands-on civic learning through participatory budgeting, community mapping, policy simulations, and direct engagement with real decision-makers.

Explore →
Front 4

Strategic Nonviolence + Leadership Development

The differentiating argument. Equipping students to understand power, navigate conflict constructively, and build collective influence. No comparison state has codified this in statute. Delaware can lead.
