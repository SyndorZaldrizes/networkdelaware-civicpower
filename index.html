The Problem

KNOWING
IS NOT
DOING

Delaware's civics standards, last revised in 2018, carry no statutory mandate, no dedicated funding, and no accountability structure. The Participation anchor, the only standard asking students to act civically is highlighted only at grade 9.

CIRCLE's 2025 research confirms: the civic engagement gap is a supply-side failure. Young people want to participate. The system isn't giving them the tools, the practice, or the invitation.

"The barrier is not motivation. It is access, safety, and structured invitation."

The Strategy

FOUR
STRATEGIC
FRONTS

The framework advances four fronts simultaneously. Every deliverable maps to at least one. Every recommendation must advance at least one. They are not sequential; they operate in parallel.

Full Framework →
// Front 01

STATEWIDE POLICY + LEA IMPLEMENTATION

Strengthen Delaware's K–12 civics expectations at the state level while supporting Local Education Agencies in alignment, educator development, and implementation with equitable accountability.

Explore → // Front 02

COMMUNITY-ROOTED POWER BUILDING

Establish parent and student leadership labs, youth organizing pathways, and feedback mechanisms that give communities real, not symbolic, influence over how civic education is designed and delivered.

Explore → // Front 03

SCHOOL-BASED PARTNERSHIPS + HANDS-ON PRACTICE

Pilot participatory budgeting, community mapping, policy simulations, and direct engagement with real decision-makers. Schools can't do this alone; community partnerships are how it scales.

Explore → // Front 04

STRATEGIC NONVIOLENCE + LEADERSHIP DEVELOPMENT

The differentiating argument. Equipping students to understand power, navigate conflict constructively, and build collective influence. No comparison state has codified this in statute. Delaware can go first.

Civic power is built through six repeating stages: Learn, Analyze, Organize, Act, Teach Back, and Reflect. The cycle never ends at action. It ends at reflection, which launches the next loop and the next cohort of civic multipliers.

Take Action Now

CIVIC
MISSIONS

You don't have to wait for a class or a program. These missions connect you to real civic processes in Delaware right now. Check them off as you complete them.

0 of 5 Complete

Find Your Representatives

Look up who represents your district in the Delaware General Assembly. What committees are they on? Have they voted on education funding?

Spot a Local Decision

Identify one recent decision made by your school board, city council, or county government. Who made it? Who was affected? Who wasn't in the room?

Draw a Power Map

Pick an issue that affects your community. Map who has decision-making authority, who is most impacted, and who could be allies in pushing for change.

Attend a Public Meeting

Find an upcoming school board, city council, or county meeting in Delaware. Attend in person or virtually. You don't have to speak. Just witness how decisions are made.

Teach Someone Else

Explain one thing you learned from this site to someone else such as a friend, family member, or classmate. When you teach, you become a civic multiplier.
